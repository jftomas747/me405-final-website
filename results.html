Track Performance

Main Track Completion Completed
Official Time 2:00

Our robot successfully completed the main required tasks of the final track, including line following, turning, wall interaction, and overall path navigation. The final system depended on the interaction between line sensing, encoder-based motion control, and inertial/state estimation during transitions where simple line following alone was not enough.

In our motor tuning and control development, the best performance came from using Kp = 2.9, Ki = 0.1, a motor gain of K = 0.2, and a set point of S = 120 counts/s.

One major challenge in tuning the robot was that changing the control values often caused unpredictable motor behavior. Small adjustments to the gains or the set point could make the response less stable, less repeatable, or more difficult to control during the final run.
