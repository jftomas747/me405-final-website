Overview

Autonomous Romi Obstacle Course Robot

Project Overview

This project brought together the major sensing, actuation, and control systems developed throughout ME405 into one autonomous Romi robot capable of navigating a printed obstacle course. Our final robot combined closed-loop motor control, encoder feedback, line following, bump sensing, inertial sensing, and observer-based state estimation into a single multitask system.

Rather than relying on only one sensing method, our design used the strengths of several subsystems together. The line sensor handled most of the path tracking, the encoders supported motion control and distance-based actions, the bump sensors handled wall interaction, and the IMU and state estimator improved heading awareness and motion prediction during turns and transitions.
